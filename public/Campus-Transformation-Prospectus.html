Campus Transformation Prospectus

Turning Closing Campuses Into Thriving Community Assets

Transform Learning · Jeff Ritter, PhD · Confidential

The closure of a college is a tragedy. The transformation of one is a beginning.

The Problem

American colleges are closing at a rate of two per week. In the last five years, over 80 private nonprofit colleges have closed or merged. Penn State is closing 7 campuses by 2027. The demographic cliff, the AI revolution, and the college loan crisis have together driven public confidence in higher education to historic lows.

When a campus closes, the community loses its workforce pipeline, a major employer, cultural and athletic facilities, and community gathering spaces. Developers buy the land and demolish the buildings.

But the buildings don't disappear. The need doesn't disappear. Only the accreditation does.

The Opportunity

A closed campus is not a failed asset — it's a mispriced asset. The buildings, land, infrastructure, and community connections are worth far more than a distressed sale price suggests. What they lack is a new operating model.

Transform Learning provides that model.

Our Fee Philosophy

Our fees are on a sliding scale and are always negotiable based on the institution's financial situation. We understand that campuses considering transformation are already under financial pressure.

  • Sliding scale based on institutional size and financial capacity
  • Revenue sharing and cooperative arrangements where appropriate — we succeed when you succeed
  • Collaborative partnerships that may include future revenue shares or performance-based compensation rather than large upfront payments
  • Grant-funded engagements where possible — many of our services qualify for federal and state workforce development grants
  • Lean operations — we keep expenses down with less travel, efficient site visits, and remote collaboration. Keeping our fees low is important to us.

Why Now

  1. Supply is increasing. 2+ colleges close per week. The inventory of available campuses is at an all-time high.
  2. Workforce demand is exploding. 11 million unfilled jobs. Employers will pay for training pipelines.
  3. Federal money is available. $81M in DOL RESTART grants. Billions in WIOA funding. EDA grants prioritizing workforce development.
  4. AI changes the economics. What used to require 15 instructors and accreditation can be delivered with a smaller team plus AI support and industry certifications.
  5. Communities are motivated. When a campus closes, local leaders want solutions. They'll partner on zoning, provide incentives, and co-invest.
