## The Problem

American colleges are closing at a rate of two per week. In Pennsylvania alone:

- **Pittsburgh Technical College** — closed August 2024. 180-acre campus in Oakdale now for sale. 165,000 sq ft of buildings, 461 dorm beds, professional kitchens, labs, sitting empty.
- **Penn State is closing 7 Commonwealth Campuses** by spring 2027 — New Kensington (72 acres, 10 buildings), Fayette (100 acres), Shenango (15 acres), DuBois, Mont Alto, Wilkes-Barre, York. Over 3,000 students displaced.
- **Triangle Tech** — closing June 2025, six locations statewide.
- **Clarks Summit University** — 92-year-old institution shuttering.
- **University of the Arts, Philadelphia** — closed 2024.

Nationally: **16 colleges closed in 2025 alone.** Hundreds more are running deficits.

When these schools close, the communities lose:
- Their primary workforce pipeline
- A major local employer
- Cultural and athletic facilities
- Community gathering spaces
- Alumni identity and pride

**But the buildings don't disappear. The need doesn't disappear. Only the accreditation does.**

---

## The Opportunity

A closed campus is not a failed asset — it's a **mispriced** asset. The buildings, land, infrastructure, and community connections are worth far more than the distressed sale price suggests. What they lack is a **new operating model**.

Transform Learning provides that model.

## The Transform Learning Campus Model

We don't buy campuses. **We partner with communities, municipalities, and owners to transform them.** Our role is strategic consulting + technology deployment + ongoing operational support.

### Phase 1: Assessment & Strategy (Months 1-3)
- Campus physical audit (buildings, condition, capacity, zoning)
- Community needs assessment (workforce gaps, demographic analysis, employer interviews)
- Financial modeling (revenue projections, capital requirements, operating costs)
- Stakeholder engagement (alumni, community leaders, local government, employers)
- **Deliverable:** Transformation Plan with 5-year financial model

### Our Fee Philosophy

Our fees are on a **sliding scale** and are always negotiable based on the institution's financial situation. We understand that campuses considering transformation are already under financial pressure — adding burdensome consulting fees defeats the purpose.

**Our approach to fees:**
- **Sliding scale** based on institutional size and financial capacity
- **Revenue sharing** and cooperative arrangements where appropriate — we succeed when you succeed
- **Collaborative partnerships** that may include equity participation, future revenue shares, or performance-based compensation rather than large upfront payments
- **Grant-funded engagements** where possible — many of our services qualify for federal and state workforce development grants, meaning the campus may pay little or nothing out of pocket
- **Lean operations** — we keep our expenses down with less travel, efficient site visits, and remote collaboration tools. Keeping our fees low is important to us because the institutions we serve are the ones that can least afford to overspend on consultants

We believe the right fee structure is one where both parties have skin in the game and both benefit from the campus's success. Every engagement is structured through honest conversation about what works for your situation.

## Why Now
